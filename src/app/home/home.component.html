Mugdha Bhagwat

“Code is like humor. When you have to explain it, it’s bad.” – Cory House

With an innate love for computers since I was about 5 years old, I gravitated towards the field of Computer Engineering and pursued a Bachelors in Technology degree, post which I joined the Software industry. Working as a Software Developer for the past 2 years I am fulfilling my passion of writing clean, scalable code to solve complex issues.

I first became interested in computer programming after playing the Road Rash game on my old PC. I opted for Computer Science vocational course during my high school (HSC) years at Ramnivas Ruia Jr. College , and built a car racing game using C++ game engine, graduating with 99% in Computer Science and 95.23% over all. With over 1M (10 lakh) students apppearing for the exam, I stood first among girls in the Mumbai division and earned a full ride scholarship for higher education from the Maharashtra State Government.

I joined the VJTI, Mumbai to pursue the BTech degree in Computer Engineering. During my 4 years of undergraduate studies I focused majorly on Cybersecurity and Blockchain domain. I parallely pursed a Diploma in Cyber Laws in India from the Asian School of Cyber Laws and the Government Law College, Mumbai. In my final year at VJTI I worked as a Research Assistant in the Blockchaina and AI lab. I published 2 research papers during that time and worked on several projects in the blockchain domain. Subsequently I was invited to conduct lectures for the Blockchain course for 3 consecutive years on Ethereum, Smart Contracts and Solidy programming. I graduated 2nd in my class with a 9.64 CPI in May 2020.

After graduation I started working at Morgan Stanlet as a Technology Analyst in August 2020 and was promoted to Sr. Technology Associate in January 2022. Since then I have worked as a Full Stack Developer leveraging technologies like Angular, Java- Spring Boot, Spark, Kafka, whilst also being involved in several activities in the firm. I worked a core team member to help organize the Code for Good hackathon to help non profits in India, I was a finalist in the internal hackathon conducted to encourage innovation within the firm.

Previously I interned at Amazon Lab126, a research and development company under Amazon, as a Software Development Engineering Intern. I leveraged React, AWS Lambas to create a solution for the Connectivity Solutions Group to help convert a manual, time consuming proccess to an autmated web based solution readily usable by the end users saving countless hours. Using the web based solution I was able to create and manage locations, clusters of devices, along with a RBAC system to prevent and decrease risks of data leakage and breaches.

During my time at Morgan Stanley and Amazon, while designing the web based solutions I was able to leverage the knowledge from my previous internship at Uxworx. Under the guidance of Mr. Saurabh Karandikar (CUA, CXA, CEO Uxworx) I worked on projects involving User Experience Analysis and Design, along with Heuristic Evalutations of websistes.
